Poll Atreides

Fantastic

The son of Duke Leto Atreides and his concubine Lady Jessica of the Bene Gesserit Order, was born on Kaladan, the ancestral planet of House Atreides.

House Atreides received the planet Arrakis, formerly in the possession of House Harkonnen, from the Padishah-Emperor Shaddam IV Corrino, and Paul went there with his mother and father. However, Baron Vladimir Harkonnen, not wanting to give up the rule of Arrakis, attacks the Atreides house and captures Leto, Paul's father, but he and his mother manage to escape.

Upon arrival at Arrakis, he was perceived by the local population, the Fremen, as the messiah (Lisan al-Gaib and Mahdi). In sitch Tabr, he took the names Usul and Muad'Dib. Under his command, the Fremen won the war for the planet Arrakis, defeating the Harkonnens and Emperor Shaddam IV.

Leto Atreides

Fantastic

Leto Atreides (in another translation of "Leto", English Leto Atreides I, 10.140 - 10.191) is a character in the novel by Frank Herbert "Dune", Paul's father. Also known as the Red Duke.

Leto is the protagonist of the Brian Herbert and Kevin Anderson trilogy, a prequel to Frank Herbert's Dune: House of Atreides, Dune: House of the Harkonnen and Dune: House of Corrino.

Lady Jessica

Fantastic

Lady Jessica (10 154 - 10 256 A.G.) [1] is a character in Frank Herbert's Dune universe, first appears in the novel "Dune" (1965), where she is one of the key characters. Concubine of Duke Leto I Atreides, mother of the protagonist of the novel by Paul Atreides, better known as Muad'Dib, a representative of the Bene Gesserit Order, possessing rare beauty, skills in fighting, hypnosis, manipulation of people and insight. Also plays an important role in the third novel of the series "Children of Dune" (1976).

The prequels to Dune, written by Frank Herbert's son Brian and co-written with Kevin Anderson, reveal the circumstances of Jessica's conception, birth and adolescence. It is indicated that Jessica's mother was the Reverend Mother Gaia Helena Mohiam (the authors of the sequel note that they found an indication of a similar idea in the notes of Frank Herbert). The character appears as a ghola in The Hunters of Dune (2006) and The Sandworms of Dune (2007).
